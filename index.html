About Me

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at BRAC University and an MSc student in Computer Science and Engineering. I am actively engaged in research in Machine Learning, with a focus on deep learning, multi-modal learning, and intelligent data-driven systems. My research interests are driven by real-world applications in healthcare analytics, secure machine learning, and socially relevant AI systems.

My research experience spans both applied and theoretical aspects of machine learning. I have worked on privacy-preserving learning frameworks using federated learning and homomorphic encryption, as well as deep learningâ€“based approaches for healthcare data analysis. In addition, my work includes natural language processing research focused on mental health and social computing, such as depressive post detection in low-resource languages. My ongoing research further explores secure healthcare systems and cognitive factors influencing security decision-making

Alongside research, I am actively involved in undergraduate teaching at BRAC University. I have taught courses including Data Structures, Algorithms, Numerical Methods, Artificial Intelligence, and Image Processing. I am particularly interested in mentoring students and integrating research-oriented thinking into coursework. This website presents a detailed overview of my research projects, publications, academic achievements, and teaching activities.
